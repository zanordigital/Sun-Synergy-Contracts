Tiles are one of the most permanent decisions in a renovation - replacing them means hacking out everything underneath, which is three to four times the cost of getting it right the first time. This guide covers tile material prices, supply-and-lay costs per square foot, and the hidden factors that push final bills higher than the quote suggests.

## Tile Price in Malaysia: Supply Only (per square foot)

These are typical market rates for tiles purchased from Malaysian suppliers and imported distributors in 2026. Prices exclude installation.

| Tile type | Price per sq ft (supply only) |
|-----------|-------------------------------|
| Standard ceramic (local brand, 30x30 to 60x60cm) | RM 2 - RM 6 |
| Porcelain (mid-range, 60x60cm) | RM 5 - RM 12 |
| Porcelain (large-format, 60x120cm or 80x80cm) | RM 10 - RM 22 |
| Full-body porcelain (marble-look, 60x120cm+) | RM 15 - RM 40 |
| Natural marble (Carrara, Nero Marquina, local marble) | RM 20 - RM 80 |
| Travertine (natural stone) | RM 18 - RM 50 |
| Mosaic (glass, stone, ceramic) | RM 15 - RM 60 |
| Encaustic / cement tile | RM 15 - RM 45 |
| Luxury vinyl tile (LVT) | RM 5 - RM 12 |

## Tiling Cost Per Square Foot (Supply and Lay)

This is the all-in cost: tile material + adhesive + grout + labour. It is the number that matters for your budget.

| Tile type | Supply and lay per sq ft |
|-----------|--------------------------|
| Standard ceramic (floor or wall) | RM 8 - RM 15 |
| Porcelain (floor, 60x60cm) | RM 14 - RM 28 |
| Large-format porcelain (floor, 80x80cm+) | RM 22 - RM 45 |
| Large-format porcelain (wall, 60x120cm+) | RM 25 - RM 55 |
| Natural marble | RM 30 - RM 90 |
| Mosaic (wall feature) | RM 30 - RM 80 |
| Herringbone or chevron pattern (any tile) | Add RM 5 - RM 12 per sq ft to the base rate |

A standard 3x4 metre living room floor (about 130 sq ft) using a mid-range 60x60 porcelain tile typically costs RM 1,800 - RM 3,600 supply and lay.

A typical Malaysian bathroom floor and wall tile job (about 120 sq ft combined) using mid-range porcelain costs RM 1,700 - RM 3,400 supply and lay, excluding the waterproofing membrane.

## What Drives the Price Up

**Tile size.** Large-format tiles (80x80 and above) need a perfectly level substrate and skilled laying to avoid lippage (uneven edges between tiles). The prep time and skill premium adds RM 5-15 per sq ft to the labour rate.

**Pattern.** A running bond (standard brick offset) is the baseline. Herringbone, chevron, or diagonal patterns generate significantly more waste and take longer to lay.

**Substrate condition.** If the existing screed is uneven or soft, it needs grinding or self-levelling compound before tiling can begin. This adds RM 3-8 per sq ft and is often only discovered when hacking starts.

**Hacking the old tiles.** If tiles are being replaced rather than laid on a bare slab, the old tiles need to be hacked out first. Budget RM 4-8 per sq ft for hacking and disposal.

**Waterproofing (wet areas).** Any floor that gets wet - bathroom, balcony, wet kitchen - needs a waterproofing membrane beneath the tile. This is not optional and costs RM 8-15 per sq ft applied, depending on membrane type. See our [wet works and plumbing service](/services/wet-works-plumbing) for more detail.

## Which Tile for Which Room?

| Room | What works | What to avoid |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| Living and dining floor | Porcelain 60x60 or larger, full-body | Polished marble (slippery when wet) |
| Bedroom floor | Porcelain, LVT, or timber laminate | Large unpolished marble (cold underfoot) |
| Kitchen floor | Matte porcelain (slip-resistant rating R10+) | Polished tiles (grease slip risk) |
| Bathroom floor | Matte mosaic or small-format porcelain (R11+) | Any polished tile |
| Bathroom wall | Any tile including large-format marble-look | Very porous natural stone (stains easily) |
| Balcony / outdoor | Anti-slip porcelain or natural stone, R12 rating | Any indoor tile (will crack under UV and water cycle) |
