Custom carpentry - wardrobes, kitchen cabinets, TV consoles, study desks, and storage units - accounts for a large share of most Malaysian home renovation budgets. Prices vary enormously depending on whether you choose a furniture retailer, a custom carpenter, or an integrated design-and-build contractor. This guide covers what bespoke built-in joinery costs from Sun Synergy Contracts, a CIDB G4-licensed renovation contractor based in Petaling Jaya, in 2026.

## Built-in Wardrobe Cost Malaysia

Wardrobe pricing is quoted per linear foot (LF) of external width, for a full floor-to-ceiling unit.

| Material grade | Cost per linear foot (floor-to-ceiling, supply and install) |
|---------------|-------------------------------------------------------------|
| Melamine (basic, 18mm local board) | RM 250 - RM 450 per LF |
| Melamine (premium, imported board, soft-close hardware) | RM 450 - RM 700 per LF |
| PVC veneer or laminate front | RM 550 - RM 800 per LF |
| Lacquer finish (spray-painted, full flush door) | RM 700 - RM 1,100 per LF |
| Solid wood veneer | RM 900 - RM 1,500 per LF |

A typical Malaysian master bedroom wardrobe running 10 feet wide, floor-to-ceiling with swing doors, soft-close hinges, and an internal layout of hanging, shelving, and drawers costs:
- **Mid-range melamine:** RM 5,000 - RM 7,500
- **Lacquer finish:** RM 8,000 - RM 12,000
- **Solid wood veneer:** RM 10,000 - RM 16,000

Walk-in wardrobe systems (island unit, perimeter shelving, internal lighting) start at RM 18,000 for a medium-specification 3x3 metre room and can reach RM 60,000+ for premium finishes with integrated lighting and a feature island.

## Kitchen Cabinet Cost Malaysia

Kitchen cabinets are priced per linear foot for base cabinets and wall cabinets separately, as the construction depth and weight-bearing requirements differ.

| Material | Base cabinet (per LF) | Wall cabinet (per LF) |
|----------|-----------------------|-----------------------|
| Melamine (local board) | RM 350 - RM 600 | RM 250 - RM 450 |
| Melamine (imported board, soft-close) | RM 600 - RM 950 | RM 450 - RM 700 |
| PVC finish | RM 750 - RM 1,100 | RM 550 - RM 850 |
| Lacquer finish | RM 950 - RM 1,500 | RM 700 - RM 1,100 |
| Solid wood (teak, oak) | RM 1,400 - RM 2,500 | RM 1,100 - RM 2,000 |

A standard Malaysian kitchen with 8 feet of base cabinets and 6 feet of wall cabinets using mid-range melamine and soft-close hardware typically costs RM 9,000 - RM 15,000 for cabinets only, excluding countertop, sink, and hob. ## What Determines the Price

**Board material.** Moisture-resistant (MR) particleboard is the baseline for Malaysian kitchens. HMR (high moisture resistant) board and imported brands such as Egger or Kronospan add RM 50-150 per linear foot but last significantly longer in humid Malaysian kitchens.

**Door finish.** Melamine (printed pattern or solid colour) is the most affordable. PVC wrapping allows curved profiles and a seamless edge. Lacquer (spray-painted) produces a premium flush look but shows fingerprints more readily. Solid wood adds warmth but requires maintenance.

**Hardware.** Soft-close hinges and drawer runners add roughly RM 15-30 per door or drawer but are strongly recommended - they protect the cabinet box joints and reduce noise. Blum and Hettich (Austrian brands) carry a premium over local hardware but come with a lifetime guarantee.

**Internal fittings.** A standard wardrobe quote typically includes a basic internal layout (1 hanging rod, 2 shelves). Pull-out drawers (RM 200-400 each), trouser racks (RM 250-500), internal LED lighting (RM 500-2,000 for a full wardrobe), and pull-out shoe racks all add to the total.

## Furniture Retailer vs Custom Carpenter vs Contractor

| Option | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|------|
| IKEA / furniture retailer | Lower cost, fast delivery | Standard sizes only; you manage installation and any fit issues |
| Local custom carpenter (direct) | Custom sizes, lower cost | Quality varies; limited accountability if something goes wrong post-installation |
| Design-and-build contractor | One contract covering cabinets, countertop, tiling, and electrical; design-led | Higher total cost than a direct carpenter; not suitable for cabinet-only jobs under RM 8,000 |
